Most "Mahjong" games you find online are not Mahjong. They are Mahjong solitaire — a single-player tile-matching puzzle with no strategy, no opponents, and nothing in common with the real game beyond the tiles. If you search "play Mahjong online," the majority of results will be solitaire variants.

Real Mahjong — four players, drawing and discarding, claiming tiles, building a winning hand — is available online, but you need to know what you are looking for.

## Real Mahjong vs Mahjong Solitaire

| | Real Mahjong | Mahjong Solitaire |
|--|-------------|-------------------|
| Players | 4 | 1 |
| Drawing/discarding | Yes | No |
| Opponents | Yes | No (or AI puzzle challenge) |
| Strategy | Deep | None |
| Learning curve | Moderate | None |
| Skill development | Yes | No |

If you want to learn or play the real game, you need a platform built for four-player Mahjong. Solitaire sites are fine entertainment, but they will not help you understand or get better at Mahjong.

---

## What to Look for in a Real Mahjong Platform

**Rule correctness.** Does the platform implement actual Mahjong rules? Can you pung, chow, kong, and call Mahjong? Is scoring handled properly? A platform that gets the rules wrong teaches you bad habits.

**Bot opponents.** Being able to play against bots means you can play any time, without needing to coordinate three other people. Essential for learning and for late-night games.

**Private rooms.** The ability to create a room and invite friends is key for playing with people you know.

**No download required.** Browser-based play is much more accessible than requiring an app install, especially for playing with friends who might be unfamiliar with the game.

**Mobile-friendly.** If you want to play on your phone, make sure the platform works well on smaller screens.

## For Riichi (Japanese) Mahjong

If you are interested in competitive Japanese Mahjong, two platforms dominate:

**Mahjong Soul** — A visually polished platform with anime aesthetics, large English-speaking player base, extensive matchmaking, and in-depth replay analysis. Free to play with cosmetic purchases. Available on desktop and mobile.

**Tenhou** — The oldest and most established Riichi platform, popular in Japan and among serious competitive players. Interface is minimal and the learning curve for the platform itself is steep. Widely respected for competitive quality.

Both implement full Riichi Mahjong rules including Dora, Furiten, and all yaku. If competitive ranked play or Riichi-specific rules are your goal, either of these will serve you well.

## For American Mahjong

American Mahjong's online presence is smaller than other variants. Most American Mahjong is played in person through clubs and community groups. The NMJL card changes annually, which makes standardised online implementation difficult.

Some mobile apps implement American Mahjong rules, but the quality and rule accuracy vary significantly. The American Mahjong community is primarily an in-person one — finding a local club is the best path for this variant.
